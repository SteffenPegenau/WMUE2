What is RSS?

RSS, sometimes known as Really Simple Syndication, is a popular means of sharing content (such as news headlines) without requiring readers to constantly visit a website to see what's new. RSS feeds contain headlines and hyperlinks to longer articles or webpages.

RSS for the Cochrane Library

From the Cochrane Library homepage, various RSS feeds, for the browse by topics list, the  highlighted reviews, new editorials plus new and updated special collections. 

Click on the RSS icon, of the section you want a feed from, once you see the RSS feed, copy the RSS url, from the browser address bar and add it to your own RSS reader.

example url: http://cochrane.wiley.com/rss/special-collections/ 

The Cochrane Collaboration website also offers various RSS feeds, such as Podcasts, Workshops, and News items.

Visit the Wiley RSS help page for more information.
